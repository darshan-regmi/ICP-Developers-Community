You are a world-class creative director, senior UI/UX designer, frontend architect, and branding strategist.

Use maximum effort adaptive thinking.

Your task is to design a highly original, minimal, modern, brutalist-inspired website for a college tech club called “ICP Developers' Community” at Informatics College Pokhara.

This is NOT an ICP blockchain website.
ICP stands for Informatics College Pokhara.

The primary goal of the website is:

- recruit new student members
- make the club feel active, elite, creative, and builder-focused
- attract developers, designers, hackers, and ambitious students
- create emotional excitement and curiosity
- avoid generic “college club” aesthetics completely

The website must feel:

- minimal but bold
- intelligent and creative
- structured yet experimental
- visually clean with strong typography
- Swiss-style inspired grid systems
- Brutalism + Neo-brutalism aesthetic
- modern developer culture energy
- subtle futuristic atmosphere
- highly intentional spacing
- smooth UX with excellent visual rhythm
- premium and memorable

The design should feel like:
“a serious creative developer movement”
not
“a generic student organization website”

Avoid:

- generic gradients everywhere
- glassmorphism overload
- startup SaaS aesthetics
- template-looking layouts
- overused blobs/shapes
- excessive animations
- cluttered sections
- fake corporate tone

The website should be highly unique and artistic while remaining extremely usable.

## WEBSITE STRUCTURE

Design the website as a conversion funnel:

1. HERO SECTION
   Purpose:
   Hook visitors emotionally within 5 seconds.

Include:

- bold statement/tagline
- short supporting line
- primary CTA (“Join the Community”)
- secondary CTA (“Explore Projects”)
- highly unique visual composition
- interactive or dynamic element
- maybe grid/noise/terminal/editor inspired visuals
- avoid cliché coding graphics

The hero must feel iconic and memorable.

---

2. ABOUT SECTION
   Explain:

- what the club is
- who it is for
- what makes it different

Keep copy concise.
Use visual storytelling rather than large paragraphs.

---

3. WHAT WE DO
   Present activities as visually distinct modules/cards:

- Workshops
- Hackathons
- Open Source
- Study Groups
- Build Sessions
- Team Projects

Make this section feel alive and interactive.

---

4. EVENTS SECTION
   Show:

- upcoming events
- previous events
- timeline or editorial layout
- RSVP interaction ideas

This section must make the club feel active and real.

---

5. PROJECTS SECTION
   This is one of the most important sections.

Showcase:

- student projects
- GitHub links
- stacks used
- screenshots/previews
- experimental layout ideas

Make projects feel like proof of skill and culture.

Avoid boring project grids.

---

6. TEAM SECTION
   Minimal but human.

Include:

- organizers
- roles
- socials
- creative hover states
- editorial photography layout ideas

Avoid traditional corporate team cards.

---

7. RESOURCES SECTION
   Include:

- learning roadmap
- curated dev resources
- GitHub organization
- beginner guides
- club onboarding

Design this section to feel useful and inviting.

---

8. JOIN / CTA SECTION
   This should feel powerful.

Include:

- Discord
- socials
- contact
- membership CTA
- strong final conversion moment

End with emotional momentum.
